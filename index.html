Know Your Dashes

Understanding the differences between punctuation marks

—

Em Dash

Longest dash, used for breaks in thought, emphasis, or to set off explanatory information

Example: "The weather today — unlike yesterday — is perfect for hiking."
❌ Often overused in AI-generated text
–

En Dash

Medium dash, used for ranges, connections, and compound adjectives

Example: "Pages 10–20" or "New York–London flight"
✅ Perfect for ranges and connections
-

Hyphen

Shortest mark, used for compound words and line breaks

Example: "Well-known author" or "Twenty-five years old"
✅ Essential for compound words
💡

Pro Tip: Most em dashes in casual writing can be replaced with commas, semicolons, or periods for better readability.
